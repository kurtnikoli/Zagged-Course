Proper Way of
Creating CTA Slide

The CTA is the most important slide in your content. Get it wrong and you lose the sale. Here's how to get it right.

📣 What Is a CTA?

A CTA means call to action. It's the moment in your content where you clearly tell people what action to take next. In our case, it's when you tell viewers where you bought the product you're talking about and guide them toward buying it too. This is one of the most important parts of the video, because without a CTA there is no direction for the viewer and no direction usually means no sales.

Bad CTA vs Good CTA

One kills your organic feel. The other keeps it alive.

Bad CTA 🚫

Breaks the
Organic Feel

A bad CTA is any CTA that breaks the organic feeling of the content. When you start mentioning things like "www", ".com", or "link in bio", the video suddenly feels like an advertisement.

This immediately damages the trust you built during the video and can ruin the whole organic strategy. The audience stops seeing it as a real recommendation and starts seeing it as a promotion.

  • ✕ Mentioning "www" or ".com"
  • ✕ Saying "link in bio"
  • ✕ Sounding like a sales pitch
  • ✕ Breaking the natural flow of the video
Good CTA ✅

Keeps It
Natural

A good CTA does the opposite. It keeps the video feeling natural, authentic, and relatable. Instead of sounding like an ad, it sounds like a real person sharing their experience.

Good CTAs often include authenticity, humor, emotion, or personal enthusiasm — which keeps the audience engaged and curious.

  • ✓ Sounds like a genuine reaction
  • ✓ Uses humor or emotion
  • ✓ Feels personal and enthusiastic
  • ✓ Makes viewers want to find the product themselves

📸 CTA Slide Comparison

See the difference side by side. Good CTAs keep it clean and bright. Bad ones feel cluttered and promotional.

Good CTA ✅
  • Product is clear
  • Has a clear CTA
  • Text is readable
Bad CTA 🚫
  • Text not readable
  • No Clear CTA
  • Just said the info about the product

✅ Good CTA Examples

These feel like a genuine reaction, not a sales pitch — which makes people much more likely to trust the recommendation and look for the product themselves.

1

"i'm purchasing this product on their website till i'm 90 years old"

Why It Works

Exaggeration signals genuine love. It's funny, personal, and makes the product sound like a must-have without any promo language.

Lifetime Loyalty
2

"it's sooooo affordable on their website"

Why It Works

Stretching the word shows real excitement. Mentions price without making it sound like a promo — feels like sharing a secret deal.

Hype + Value
3

"the only bad thing is that this product is always sold out on their website"

Why It Works

A "complaint" that's actually a flex. Scarcity + demand signals make the product feel exclusive and desirable.

Reverse Psychology
TL;DR

Sound Like a Fan,
Not a Salesperson

The best CTAs don't feel like CTAs. They feel like a real person who's genuinely excited about something they found.
